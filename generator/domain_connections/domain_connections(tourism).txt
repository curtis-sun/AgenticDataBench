# Tourism Domain — Cross-Folder Data Connection Guide

## Data Source Overview

### Airbnb_Open_Data.csv
~103K listings of short-term rentals in New York City. Each row is one listing.

- **Geography**: neighbourhood group, neighbourhood, lat, long, country, country code.
- **Listing**: NAME, room type (Private room / Entire home/apt / Shared room), Construction year, instant_bookable.
- **Pricing**: price, service fee (string with "$" and space — need cleaning).
- **Reviews**: number of reviews, last review (date), reviews per month, review rate number (1–5).
- **Host**: host id, host name, host_identity_verified, calculated host listings count.
- **Availability**: minimum nights, availability 365, cancellation_policy.

### FISETIO … /datasets/
UK attraction visitor data with weather, covering museums (indoor) and parks (outdoor) monthly from 2010–2019.

- **indoors.csv** (~2.7K rows): date (YYYY-MM), museum, visits, tmax, tmin, air_frost_days, rain, sunny_hr, rating, revs, mus_ID.
- **outdoors.csv** (~2.9K rows): date, y (visits), temp, tempmax, tempmin, tempdif, pcp, rev, rate, park_id, park_name.
- **indoors_list_museums.csv** (27 rows): ID, name, TA_link, city, fee (free/paid), climate_station. Lookup table for indoors.csv via mus_ID ↔ ID.

### TripAdvisor Indonesia Tourism/
Normalized relational dataset of tourist attraction visits in Indonesia. 9 Excel files forming a star schema.

- **Fact table**: Transaction.xlsx (~53K rows) — TransactionId, UserId, VisitYear, VisitMonth, VisitMode, AttractionId, Rating.
- **Dimensions**:
  - User.xlsx (~33.5K rows): UserId, ContenentId, RegionId, CountryId, CityId.
  - Item.xlsx (30 rows): AttractionId, AttractionCityId, AttractionTypeId, Attraction, AttractionAddress.
  - Type.xlsx (17 rows): AttractionTypeId, AttractionType.
  - Mode.xlsx (6 rows): VisitModeId, VisitMode.
  - City.xlsx (~9.1K rows): CityId, CityName, CountryId.
  - Country.xlsx (165 rows): CountryId, Country, RegionId.
  - Region.xlsx (22 rows): Region, RegionId, ContentId.
  - Continent.xlsx (6 rows): ContenentId, Contenent.

## Cross-Source Connection Points

### Connection 1: Rating / Review Score
- Airbnb: review rate number (1–5), number of reviews, reviews per month.
- FISETIO indoors: rating, revs; outdoors: rate, rev.
- TripAdvisor: Transaction.Rating.
- All three measure visitor satisfaction on similar scales, enabling cross-sector comparison (accommodation vs attraction).

### Connection 2: Time Dimension
- FISETIO: date (YYYY-MM), range 2010–2019.
- TripAdvisor: VisitYear + VisitMonth, similar range.
- Airbnb: last review (date), Construction year.
- Monthly granularity aligns between FISETIO and TripAdvisor for temporal trend analysis.

### Connection 3: Geography (conceptual, not direct join)
- Airbnb: New York City (lat/long, neighbourhood).
- FISETIO: UK cities (city column in museum lookup — London, Edinburgh, etc.).
- TripAdvisor: Indonesia (hierarchical: Continent → Region → Country → City).
- No direct geographic overlap, but Country.xlsx contains 165 countries (potential bridge to external data). City-level and country-level analyses can be compared across sources.

### Connection 4: Visitor Volume / Demand
- FISETIO: monthly visits (visits / y columns) — actual footfall counts.
- TripAdvisor: Transaction count per attraction per month — proxy for visit volume.
- Airbnb: number of reviews and availability 365 — proxy for booking demand.

## Suggested Cross-Source Analysis Patterns

1. **Rating Distribution Comparison**: Compare rating/review score distributions across the three data sources (Airbnb listings vs UK museums vs Indonesian attractions). Analyze whether accommodation ratings follow different patterns than attraction ratings.

2. **Seasonality Analysis**: Use FISETIO monthly visits and TripAdvisor (VisitYear, VisitMonth) to detect seasonal peaks. Compare with Airbnb review activity (last review dates) to identify whether accommodation demand aligns with attraction visit peaks.

3. **Weather–Visitor Correlation (FISETIO internal)**: Regression or correlation analysis between weather variables (temp, rain, sunny_hr) and visitor counts for museums vs parks. Indoor attractions may show inverse weather sensitivity compared to outdoor ones.

4. **Star Schema Drill-Down (TripAdvisor internal)**: Join all 9 dimension/fact tables to analyze rating patterns by attraction type, visit mode, user origin continent/region, and city. Practice multi-table join and dimensional modeling.

5. **Pricing and Demand Analysis (Airbnb)**: Clean price/service fee strings, analyze pricing by room type, neighbourhood group, and cancellation policy. Correlate price with review rate and availability to model price–demand relationships.

6. **Cross-Domain Tourism Profile**: Aggregate each source to its highest common granularity (e.g., monthly or yearly averages) and build a comparative tourism profile — average rating, visit volume trend, and demand seasonality — across NYC accommodation, UK attractions, and Indonesian attractions.
