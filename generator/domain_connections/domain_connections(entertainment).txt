# Entertainment Domain — Cross-Source Data Connection Guide

## Data Source Overview

### spotify_tracks.csv
~114,000 Spotify tracks across 114 genres. Each row has audio features (danceability, energy, loudness, speechiness, acousticness, instrumentalness, liveness, valence, tempo) plus metadata (track_id, artists, album_name, track_name, popularity, track_genre, explicit).

### top2017.csv / top2018.csv / top50_2020.csv
Year-end top songs for 2017 (~100), 2018 (~100), and 2020 (50). Share audio feature columns with spotify_tracks but use slightly different naming: top2017/2018 have "id"/"name"/"artists", top50_2020 has "track_id"/"track_name"/"artist"/"genre"/"album". Harmonization needed before merging.

### The Movies Dataset/
TMDb + GroupLens multi-file movie database. Internal join key: TMDb "id" across movies_metadata/credits/keywords; GroupLens "movieId" in ratings; links.csv bridges tmdbId↔movieId↔imdbId.

- **movies_metadata.csv**: ~45K movies with title, genres (JSON), release_date, budget, revenue, runtime, popularity, vote_average, vote_count, overview, production_companies (JSON), production_countries (JSON).
- **credits.csv**: cast/crew per movie (JSON lists requiring parsing).
- **keywords.csv**: plot keywords per movie (JSON list).
- **ratings.csv / ratings_small.csv**: User-movie ratings (0.5–5.0). ratings.csv has ~26M rows; ratings_small.csv (~100K) is a manageable subset.
- **links.csv / links_small.csv**: ID mapping between GroupLens movieId, IMDb imdbId, and TMDb tmdbId.

### vgsales.csv
~16,600 video games from VGChartz. Columns: Name, Platform, Year, Genre, Publisher, and regional sales (NA_Sales, EU_Sales, JP_Sales, Other_Sales, Global_Sales in millions). Year has some missing values.

### netflix_titles.csv
~8,800 Netflix titles (movies and TV shows). Columns: type ("Movie"/"TV Show"), title, director, cast, country, date_added, release_year, rating (content rating like PG-13/TV-MA, NOT quality score), duration, listed_in (genres, comma-separated), description. Multi-valued columns (cast, country, listed_in) need splitting.

### books.csv
~11,100 Goodreads books. Columns: title, authors (slash-separated), average_rating (1–5), language_code, num_pages, ratings_count, text_reviews_count, publication_date, publisher.

## Cross-Source Connection Points

### Connection 1: Genre / Category (semantic match)
- spotify_tracks "track_genre", movies_metadata "genres" (JSON), vgsales "Genre", netflix_titles "listed_in", top50_2020 "genre".
- Taxonomies differ across media — requires semantic mapping (e.g., movie "Action" ≈ game "Action"; music genres have no direct film equivalent).

### Connection 2: Title / Name (fuzzy match for adaptations/franchises)
- movies_metadata "title", netflix_titles "title", books "title", vgsales "Name".
- Links book→movie adaptations, movie→game tie-ins, TMDb↔Netflix overlaps. Requires fuzzy string matching.

### Connection 3: Year / Time (direct match, ~1980–2020 overlap)
- movies_metadata "release_date", vgsales "Year", netflix_titles "release_year"/"date_added", books "publication_date", top songs implicitly tagged (2017/2018/2020).

### Connection 4: Rating / Popularity (scale normalization needed)
- movies_metadata "vote_average" (1–10) + ratings.csv (0.5–5.0), spotify_tracks "popularity" (0–100), vgsales "Global_Sales" (millions, as popularity proxy), books "average_rating" (1–5).
- Note: netflix_titles "rating" is content rating (PG-13, TV-MA), NOT a quality score.

### Connection 5: Creator / Person (fuzzy match)
- spotify_tracks "artists", credits.csv cast/crew names, netflix_titles "director"/"cast", books "authors".
- Some creators span media (actors who are musicians, authors whose books become films).

### Connection 6: Country / Region
- netflix_titles "country", movies_metadata "production_countries" (JSON), vgsales regional sales (NA/EU/JP/Other), books "language_code" as region proxy.

## Suggested Cross-Source Analysis Patterns

### Within Music
1. **Hit Song Evolution**: Compare audio feature distributions across top2017, top2018, top50_2020 to detect year-over-year shifts in hit characteristics; use spotify_tracks as baseline.
2. **Genre Audio Fingerprinting**: Cluster spotify_tracks by audio features; analyze which genres are acoustically distinct vs. overlapping.

### Within Movies
3. **Revenue Prediction**: Parse JSON columns from movies_metadata, join with credits.csv for director/cast features, build regression predicting revenue from budget, genre, cast, and timing.
4. **Collaborative Filtering**: Build user-user or item-item recommendation from ratings_small.csv; enrich with movies_metadata content features.

### Cross-Media (2+ sources)
5. **Cross-Media Genre Trends**: Map genres to common taxonomy across movies, games, and Netflix; compare popularity rankings by year to detect synchronized trends.
6. **Book-to-Movie Adaptation**: Fuzzy-match book titles to movie/Netflix titles; compare book average_rating with movie vote_average (after normalization).
7. **Regional Taste Profiling**: Compare Japan game preferences (JP_Sales by Genre) with Japan-origin Netflix content and Japanese-language books for cross-media regional patterns.
8. **Publisher/Studio Market Concentration**: Compare top publishers in vgsales (by sales), production companies in movies (by revenue), and book publishers (by ratings_count); compute market concentration metrics.
9. **Content Rating vs. Reception**: Match netflix_titles to movies_metadata by title+year; analyze whether mature-rated content receives systematically different audience scores.
10. **Multi-Media Temporal Trends**: Count releases per year across movies, games, Netflix additions, and books; identify cross-media boom/bust periods.

### 3+ Sources
11. **Entertainment Franchise Lifecycle**: Trace major franchises (Harry Potter, Star Wars, Marvel) across books, movies, games, and streaming via title matching; compare cross-media popularity metrics.
12. **Creator Crossover Analysis**: Find individuals appearing across music (artists), movies (cast/crew), Netflix (director/cast), and books (authors); analyze whether cross-media presence correlates with higher popularity.
